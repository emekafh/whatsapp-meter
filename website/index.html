Frequently asked questions

Do I need the regular WhatsApp or WhatsApp Business?
You need WhatsApp Business (the free app with a briefcase icon). The regular WhatsApp app doesn't support the Cloud API that WhatsApp Meter uses. If you're currently using regular WhatsApp, you can migrate to WhatsApp Business without losing any chats — it's a free, one-time switch.
Will this affect how my WhatsApp works?
Not at all. WhatsApp Meter uses the "Coexistence" feature, which means your WhatsApp Business app continues working exactly as before. The app just listens to message events in the background — it doesn't send messages or change any settings on your phone.
Can other people see my response times?
No. Everything stays on your computer. There are no accounts, no cloud sync, no sharing features. Your data is yours alone — it's stored in a local database file that only you can access.
Does it actually read my messages?
No. WhatsApp Meter only records timestamps and sender information. The actual message text, photos, voice notes, and all other content are completely ignored. You can verify this yourself — the code is open-source.
What's a Meta Developer account? Is it hard to set up?
It's a free account at developers.facebook.com — you just log in with your existing Facebook account. The setup wizard in the app walks you through creating a "WhatsApp App" there step by step, with screenshots and instructions for each click. The whole process takes about 3–4 minutes.
How far back does the data go?
The Cloud API's Coexistence feature auto-syncs up to 6 months of message history. For older data, you can export chats from WhatsApp as .txt files and import them into the app — just drag and drop.
Does the app need to stay running?
Yes, the app runs quietly in your system tray (the small icons area near your clock). When you close the window, it minimises to the tray so the webhook listener stays active and keeps collecting data. You can quit it from the tray icon whenever you want.
